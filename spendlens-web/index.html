Three traps most MMM builds fall into

SpendLens is built specifically to avoid these — not as a slogan, but as measurable design choices.

Trap 01 — Leakage

Validating on the past

A random train/test split lets a model "see" next month while being scored on last month — invisible in the demo, fatal in production.

—
Trap 02 — Collinearity

Manual VIF pruning

Ad channels move together (shared promo calendars). Plain regression gives unstable coefficients that flip which channel "wins" week to week.

—
Trap 03 — Silent drift

A model marketers can't audit

An automated weekly retrain that silently changes its mind about a channel erodes trust fast — and nobody's watching a notebook.

—

What's actually under the hood

◐

Adstock + saturation, one pipeline

Geometric decay and Hill saturation are sklearn-native transformers, tuned jointly with the ElasticNet penalty — not hand-fit before regression.

▤

Expanding-window CV

Walk-forward validation only. Training window grows forward in time, matching exactly how the model is used in production.

◈

Validated against ground truth

Synthetic data with known adstock/saturation parameters lets us prove the pipeline recovers the truth, not just fits noise.

⚠

Automated drift monitoring

Every retrain compares channel coefficients to the prior run and flags swings past a configurable threshold — the marketer's trust signal.

∑

Constrained optimization

SLSQP solves a real concave-maximization problem for next week's allocation, respecting per-channel min/max spend bounds.

✓

Controls, not just spend

Price, discount, stock-outs, delivery delays, and macro demand are modeled explicitly — so media never gets credit for someone else's effect.
